Barrick vs Newmont — did they produce the gold they guided?

The companion to the capex study: they told the market how much gold they'd produce each year — here is the guidance range vs the actual, on each company's own attributable basis, FY2015–2025, plus the forward 2026–2028 trajectory. Every number is scripted from primary results releases; quotes are verbatim and source-linked. The answer: production delivery is the SAME story as capex — both miners hit their final guide almost every year, but they get there by revising it down mid-year, and both systematically come in under their original estimate. And the forward read connects to the capex: Newmont calls 2026 a production trough (recovering toward ~6 Moz from 2027), while Barrick's 2026 is roughly flat on a continuing-ops basis (its reported decline to ~3.1 Moz is mostly the Hemlo/Tongon divestitures) before stepping up to 3.4–3.75 Moz by 2028 — the output the heavy 2026 capex is built to deliver.
