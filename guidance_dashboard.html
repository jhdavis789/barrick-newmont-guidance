Barrick vs Newmont — what they guided on capex vs what they spent, and where they point now

Ten years of forward capex guidance (the range each company set at the start of the year) laid against the actual spend, then the FY2026 signal. Each company is compared on its own basis (Barrick: attributable; Newmont: consolidated) — guidance and actual on the same footing. Every number is scripted from primary results releases; quotes are verbatim and source-linked (the adversarial verify pass dropped 2 fabricated quotes and re-attributed several). The story today: Barrick is ramping into its biggest build in over a decade; Newmont is holding flat and returning cash.

The forward signal today (FY2026)

Where each management is pointing right now, from the Feb-2026 FY2025 results and the Q1-2026 calls. The split is as telling as the total: 62% of Barrick's 2026 guide is GROWTH (project) capital — its single biggest line is the ~$1.6–1.75B copper project budget (led by Lumwana ~$0.75–0.85B and Reko Diq ~$0.6–0.7B at 50%, plus Zaldívar/Jabal Sayid) — versus just 42% growth for Newmont. Neither miner guides capex beyond 2026, but Barrick's 2026 build is timed to bring Reko Diq/Lumwana online — its production outlook rises to 3.40–3.75 Moz by 2028 (+~16% on guidance midpoints; Lumwana first copper Q1-2028, though Reko Diq's first production is now under a security review extended to mid-2027). Both are returning record cash at ~$4,300 gold — Barrick returned a record $2.39B in 2025 plus a new $3.0B buyback; Newmont returned $3.4B with $2.4B of buyback left — so it's a tilt, not a binary: Barrick leans to build, Newmont to harvest.

Further out — what they've signalled for 2027 and beyond

Neither company guides capex past 2026 (Barrick's capex table is single-year-forward; Newmont guides only 2026 and flags sustaining capex staying elevated "over the next few years"). The further-out guidance is production- and project-based:
Barrick
  • Production outlook: 3.30–3.65 Moz gold (2027), 3.40–3.75 Moz (2028) + copper 195–225 / 255–285 kt — Q1-2026 three-year outlook
  • Lumwana Super Pit first copper end of Q1-2028 (~240kt/yr, 30+ yr life); Goldrush ramping to ~400koz/yr by 2028; Fourmile underground from 2027
  • Reko Diq first production now under a security review extended to mid-2027 — "could be significant increases" to capex/timeline (Apr-2026)
  • Strategy: +30% gold-equivalent output by ~2030 (directional); a North-American gold-asset IPO targeted late 2026; new $3B buyback (May-2026)
Newmont
  • Production: 2026 is a self-described trough (~5.3 Moz); directional recovery from 2027 toward a ~6 Moz + 150kt copper longer-term anchor (no specific 2027/28 Moz range published)
  • Tanami Expansion 2 commercial H2-2027 ($1.7–1.8B); Lihir Nearshore Barrier H2-2028 (unlocks >5 Moz); Cadia PC1-2 cave to 2029; Boddington grades up 2027
  • Red Chris block-cave FID targeted H2-2026; reclamation cash normalises to $300–400M/yr from 2028 (vs ~$850M in 2025–27)
  • Capital return: ~$1.1B/yr dividend + a $6B buyback authorization

Guidance band vs actual — the 10-year track record

Two questions, two answers. (1) Did they hit their final guide? — actual vs the guidance in force at year-end (revised if revised). Both basically do: Barrick 7/11, Newmont 8/9. (2) Did they spend what they first estimated? — actual vs the initial guidance midpoint. Here they diverge: Barrick lands ~on target (ex its 2015–16 deleveraging cuts), while Newmont spends ~8% under its original number, because it walks the guide down mid-year (5 of 7 revisions were cuts). Shaded = the range guided (point ± ~5% where a single number is given); line = actual; ✦ = 2026 guide. M&A-reset years (Newmont/Goldcorp 2019, Newmont/Newcrest 2023) and Newmont's 2024 basis-gap year are flagged and excluded from the deviation stat.

Barrick — guidance vs actual

Attributable total capital, $B. Shaded = INITIAL guidance range; teal ▽ = revised guide where issued; line = actual; ✦ = 2026 guide. Barrick gives a range and mostly lands in it — 7/11 within its final guide; vs its original estimate it averaged just -1.5% ex the deliberate 2015–16 deleveraging cuts (those two years alone were ~−26%/−25%; note 2025 was −10% and IS in that ex-cuts mean). The 3 misses are real (2015–16 cuts + 2025); 2022 the lone overshoot — it does not game its guidance. The 2026 ✦ steps up hard — the biggest capex Barrick has guided in a decade (it guides capex only one year out, so there is no 2027 number; ~$0.6–0.7B of the 2026 figure, Reko Diq, is under a security review extended to mid-2027).

Newmont — guidance vs actual

Consolidated total capital, $B. Newmont guided ranges through 2018 (and again in 2023), and single points in 2019–2022 and 2024–25. Shaded = INITIAL guidance; the teal ▽ shows where it walked the guide to — that's why in 2016/2020/2025 the actual sits below the shaded initial band yet inside the final guide. It almost always hits that final guide (8/9 within) by revising down: 5 of 7 revisions cut the number (the 2022 cut was only ~$25M/1%). Versus its original estimate it spent -8.0% (or -6.3% ex its 2020 COVID deferral; its other deep miss, 2018, was largely a Tanami power capital-lease that never hit the PP&E line). ±5% point band is company-stated from 2025, applied back; within-count is 4/9 strict (range years scored actual-in-range, point years exact), 7 at ±3%, 8 at ±5%, 8 at ±10% — 2019 (a 4.4% gap) is the only year that flips between ±3% and ±5% (three other point years move between strict and ±3%). 2019/2023/2024 excluded from the deviation stat; 2019 still counts in the hit-rate (its revised guide already reflected Goldcorp).
